US export controls force Anthropic to take Fable 5 and Mythos 5 offline globally

A Commerce Department directive delivered June 12 at 5:21 pm ET ordered Anthropic to cut access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all foreign nationals — including its own non-citizen staff. Anthropic disabled both models globally; Claude Opus 4.8 and all other Claude models remain live.

Why it matters — The first time US export controls have been applied to a deployed AI model — every frontier lab now has a live precedent to plan around.

AI Research & Models

Anthropic contests the order, warns a single-jailbreak standard would freeze new deployments

The directive followed a claimed jailbreak of Mythos 5. Anthropic says its review found only previously known minor vulnerabilities and that identical prompts produce comparable outputs from public models including GPT-5.5, arguing that applying this standard industry-wide would halt frontier model launches.

Why it matters — If a single demonstrated jailbreak can trigger global suspension, every lab's release calculus changes overnight.

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 shutdown forces developers to fall back to Opus 4.8

With Fable 5 and Mythos 5 suspended, teams that had moved agentic, long-context, and coding workloads onto them need an immediate continuity plan; Anthropic confirmed Claude Opus 4.8 and the rest of its model lineup remain fully available.

VS Code 1.124 enables Copilot Autopilot by default for all users

Released June 10, VS Code 1.124 turns Autopilot on globally and introduces Advanced Autopilot — a secondary utility model that evaluates whether a task is truly finished before the primary agent stops — plus background agent sessions and keyboard-driven session navigation.

Why it matters — Most VS Code users are now running autonomous agent loops by default, whether or not they opted in.

Visual Studio Magazine

Cognition retires Windsurf brand, ships Devin Desktop with multi-agent coordination

Cognition relaunched the Windsurf IDE as Devin Desktop on June 7, introducing Spaces for grouping agent activity by project and native Agent Client Protocol (ACP) support that lets Codex, Claude Agent, OpenCode, and other third-party agents run inside the same environment alongside Devin.

Why it matters — The first major IDE to treat agent coordination — not just code editing — as a first-class primitive.

Apple ships Foundation Models with cloud routing and open-source plans at WWDC 2026

Wrapping up WWDC this week, Apple announced Foundation Models now routes to third-party models — Claude, Gemini, GPT — through a unified Swift API, adds image input, and introduces Dynamic Profiles for multi-agent workflows. Free Private Cloud Compute access extends to apps under two million first-time downloads, and the framework will go open source later this summer.

Why it matters — A unified on-device and cloud inference API — plus the open-source commitment — could significantly lower the barrier for iOS/macOS AI features.

GitHub Copilot usage-based billing takes effect June 1

Copilot replaced Premium Request Units with token-metered AI Credits ($0.01 each). Code completions stay unlimited, but chat, agentic sessions, and code review now consume credits that do not roll over — with some heavy agentic users reporting monthly bills jumping from $39 to over $800.

Why it matters — Worth auditing team token spend now; agentic coding at scale just became materially more expensive.

BTC is trading around $63,858 on June 13, stabilizing after an 18% drop triggered by Strategy's first bitcoin sale since 2022 and $4.37 billion in spot ETF redemptions across 13 consecutive trading days through June 3. The Fear & Greed Index remains at 12.

The Block

CLARITY Act moves to Senate floor calendar with eight weeks before summer recess

The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act — which would grant the CFTC exclusive jurisdiction over digital commodity spot markets while maintaining SEC oversight of investment contract assets — was placed on the full Senate calendar June 2 after clearing the Banking Committee 15-9. The bill still needs 60 floor votes plus House reconciliation before any recess.

Why it matters — The clearest path to a US federal crypto market-structure law in years — but the clock is tight.

CoinDesk

CFTC approves first US-regulated bitcoin perpetual futures on KalshiEX

The CFTC greenlighted KalshiEX's BTCPERP contract on May 29 — the first perpetual futures listing on a US-regulated exchange. The contract retains the offshore funding-rate mechanism but adds US-standard margin requirements and position limits absent from offshore venues.

Why it matters — Pulls one of crypto's largest trading instruments onshore into a regulated framework for the first time.
