Binance suspends EU services as MiCA enforcement arrives: world's largest exchange locked out of Europe

Effective July 1, Binance is halting new orders, deposits, sign-ups, and staking products for EU residents after failing to secure a CASP license under the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation. The exchange withdrew its Greek application on June 24 ahead of an expected rejection citing fit-and-proper concerns around co-founder Changpeng Zhao and historical AML penalties; it now plans to seek authorization in France. Existing user funds remain safe and withdrawable.

Why it matters — The world's largest crypto exchange by volume is removed from the European market on MiCA's first enforcement day — the most significant regulatory market exit in crypto since the GDPR era.

AI for Software Development

Copilot's first usage-based billing month closes: agentic workflows confirmed at 5–10x flat-rate costs

June 30 marks the end of GitHub Copilot's inaugural token-metered billing cycle, which replaced unlimited access on June 1. Developers running agentic sessions — code review, multi-file edits, parallel agent loops — are seeing confirmed monthly charges of $750–$3,000, versus the $39 Pro+ flat rate. The official community thread accumulated 400-plus comments and nearly 900 downvotes when the switch went live.

Why it matters — The first full monthly bill landing today gives teams real data to decide whether to cap Copilot usage, switch to OpenCode or Cursor, or absorb the higher cost.

AI Research & Models

Qualcomm closes $3.9B Modular acquisition, giving AI developers Mojo and MAX on Snapdragon

Qualcomm confirmed the close of its all-stock acquisition of Modular for $3.92 billion on June 26, gaining roughly 150 engineers, the Mojo programming language (a Python superset built for AI/ML performance), and the MAX graph compiler and inference engine. The MAX stack already runs cross-vendor across Nvidia, AMD, Intel, and Arm hardware; Qualcomm's goal is making its Snapdragon and Dragonfly data-center chips viable AI inference targets without needing CUDA.

Why it matters — First credible full-stack CUDA alternative with major chip-vendor backing; could meaningfully expand the non-Nvidia hardware surface for AI model deployment.

GitHub Copilot for Jira reaches general availability with real-time agent progress streaming

GitHub made its Copilot coding agent integration for Jira generally available on June 25, following a March public preview. The GA release adds real-time progress streaming to Jira tickets — status updates appear in the issue as the agent works — plus model selection, Confluence context via MCP, custom agents, and follow-up instruction delivery from the Jira chat panel after the agent opens a draft pull request.

Why it matters — Closes the loop between issue tracking and autonomous code generation in a single workflow, without leaving the project management tool.

Strategy unveils Digital Credit Capital Framework, authorizes up to $1.25B in Bitcoin sales

MicroStrategy (now Strategy) announced a 'Digital Credit Capital Framework' on June 29, departing from its passive accumulation model. The framework authorizes $2 billion in stock repurchases and a Bitcoin monetization program allowing up to $1.25 billion in BTC sales to fund preferred dividends and debt repurchases. The company still holds approximately 843,738 BTC; it made its first Bitcoin sale in company history in late May (32 BTC).

Why it matters — Strategy treating its ~$50B BTC stack as a flexible capital resource — not a one-way accumulation trade — marks a structural shift in the single largest corporate Bitcoin holder's posture.

Bloomberg

White House sets July 22 deadline for US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve architecture

White House Digital Assets Advisory Council executive director Bo Hines confirmed the Trump administration will release the design for a US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve before a July 22 inter-agency regulatory report deadline. The US currently holds roughly 328,000 BTC (~$20B) via law-enforcement seizures. The reserve design will determine whether the government actively acquires additional Bitcoin or simply retains existing holdings under a formal custody framework.

Why it matters — July 22 is the first concrete policy gate that could make the US a systematic Bitcoin buyer — outcome will materially affect long-term supply dynamics and institutional positioning.
