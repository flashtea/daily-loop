Bitcoin

Warsh's hawkish FOMC debut withholds dot, majority project 2026 rate hike — BTC falls to $63k

The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75% on June 17 but issued its most hawkish dot plot in two years: nine of eighteen officials now project at least one hike before year-end, while Chair Warsh declined to submit his own dot and stripped forward-guidance language from the statement entirely. Bitcoin fell from $65,800 to near $63,000 in the hours after the announcement; CME FedWatch now prices a 61% chance of an October hike.

Why it matters — Warsh's deliberate ambiguity removes the 'rates-going-lower' backstop that underpinned BTC's 2025–26 rally; the October meeting is now the key near-term catalyst for direction.

Claude Design overhauled: design system imports, token-burn fix, and bidirectional Claude Code handoff

Anthropic shipped a major rebuild of Claude Design on June 17, adding the ability to import design systems from a GitHub repo, design files, or raw uploads — Claude then builds against those components and auto-corrects deviations before output. The update resolves the token-burn problem that burned through a Pro allowance in 25 minutes during the April launch, and closes a full design-to-code round-trip via a new /design command in Claude Code.

Why it matters — Design-to-code teams can now enforce brand systems end-to-end without leaving the Anthropic toolchain; admin controls let orgs lock down a single approved design standard.

Claude (Anthropic)

Claude Code v1.11 patches WSL2 scrolling, Vim undo, and mid-stream connection drops

The June 17 Claude Code release fixes mouse-wheel scrolling in WSL2 under Windows Terminal and VS Code, restores Vim-mode undo to step through commands one at a time instead of merging them, and prevents mid-stream disconnects from stranding the spinner at 'running tool'. Plugin loading performance, remote session task states, and subagent viewing were also improved.

Why it matters — WSL2 scrolling and Vim undo were the top-reported usability blockers from Windows and keyboard-driven users.

Dev Tools & Releases

Gemini CLI is dead as of today; developers blast Google over open-source bait-and-switch

Google terminated Gemini CLI and Gemini Code Assist for all free, Pro, and Ultra users on June 18, directing them to Antigravity CLI — a closed-source replacement. Critics note Google accepted 6,000+ community pull requests over the tool's life then restricted its future to paying enterprise customers; early Antigravity users also report its compute cap is far tighter than Gemini CLI's 1,000 daily requests, with some exhausting it in two requests.

Why it matters — Any solo developer or small team running Gemini CLI lost access this morning; Standard and Enterprise licensees are unaffected but should test the new tooling.

Google Developers Blog

Google AI Studio adds native Android app building via Gemini 3.5 Flash

Starting June 17, developers can build native Android apps directly in AI Studio's Build tab using Gemini 3.5 Flash, without switching to Android Studio. The feature scaffolds, iterates, and debugs Android Kotlin projects from a single prompt interface using Gemini's agentic coding capabilities.

Why it matters — Lowers the Android development barrier for web and AI-first teams that have traditionally avoided the Android Studio toolchain.

On-chain bottom signal: long-term holders absorbed 125,000 BTC in June — one of the cycle's largest accumulation months

On-chain data published June 17 shows wallets holding BTC for more than 155 days absorbed roughly 125,000 coins in June — a historically significant accumulation rate that has coincided with cycle lows in prior bear phases. Bitcoin is currently sitting approximately 50% below its October all-time high.

Why it matters — Long-term holder accumulation at this scale has historically preceded multi-month recoveries, though the newly hawkish Fed complicates the timing.

Kraken launches CFTC-regulated bitcoin perpetual futures for US traders via Bitnomial

Kraken enabled perpetual futures trading for eligible US clients through Bitnomial — a CFTC-licensed designated contract market it acquired earlier this year — making it the first major exchange to bring regulated crypto perps onshore. Nine assets are covered at launch including BTC and ETH; perpetuals globally generated over $60 trillion in volume in 2025.

Why it matters — US traders can now access the world's highest-volume crypto derivative without routing through offshore venues — a structural market milestone that follows the CFTC's May framework.
