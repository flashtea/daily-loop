AI Research & Models

The White House starts dictating who gets access to frontier AI models

The Trump administration has begun directly deciding which companies and government entities can access the most capable models from Anthropic and OpenAI, a decision that until now sat with the labs themselves; it follows a June executive order asking AI companies to give the government early access to models for testing.

Why it matters — It's a real shift in leverage from the AI labs to the federal government over who can use the most powerful models, arriving the same week a Chinese open-weight model narrowed the capability gap.

Google delays Gemini 3.5 Pro again after it misses internal coding targets

Google's flagship Gemini 3.5 Pro remains months behind its original schedule after the model fell short of internal expectations on coding and complex reasoning tasks; the company reset its training data in June to address the gap but results were reportedly still disappointing.

Why it matters — Google is now the one flagship lab visibly behind on coding capability, just as Anthropic, OpenAI and open-weight Chinese labs all ship competitive coding models.

Bloomberg

VS Code 1.129 ships a dedicated agent host and a new Agents editor panel

The July release of Visual Studio Code adds a dedicated background process for running agent sessions that multiple windows can connect to, a new experimental editor panel for reviewing agent-generated diffs, the ability to run terminal commands from chat with a "!" prefix, and a preview of a redesigned workbench UI.

Why it matters — It's Microsoft further restructuring the editor itself around long-running, multi-window agent sessions rather than single inline suggestions.

Indian vibe-coding startup Emergent becomes a unicorn a year after launch

Emergent raised a $130 million Series C led by Creaegis at a $1.5 billion valuation, a five-fold jump in six months; the AI-agent app-building platform now has an annualized run rate of $120 million and more than 200,000 paying customers.

Why it matters — It's one of the fastest unicorn runs of the year, and a sign investor appetite for AI app-building tools hasn't cooled despite a crowded field.

AI Research & Models

Thinking Machines ships Inkling, its first open-weight model

Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab released Inkling, a 975-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model (41 billion active parameters) that reasons over text, images and audio with a 1-million-token context window, plus a smaller preview variant called Inkling-Small.

Why it matters — It's the company's first public model release and adds another credible open-weight frontier option alongside this month's Chinese releases.

Thinking Machines Lab

OpenAI says automated red-teaming model GPT-Red keeps cutting jailbreak success rates

OpenAI detailed GPT-Red, an internal-only automated red-teaming model trained at large compute scale that generates attacks during training to harden production models; the company says GPT-5.6 Sol now fails on only 0.05% of GPT-Red's direct prompt-injection attempts.

Why it matters — It's a concrete example of using models to find and close their own successors' safety gaps before release, rather than relying only on human red-teamers.

Dev Tools & Releases

Microsoft's July Patch Tuesday fixes a record 570 flaws, including 3 zero-days

Microsoft's largest Patch Tuesday ever addressed 570 vulnerabilities, 59 of them critical, including three zero-days (two already exploited in the wild); Microsoft credited an internal AI-driven vulnerability-scanning system with helping surface the unusually high volume.

Why it matters — Two of the zero-days are already under active attack, so unpatched Windows and SharePoint deployments are an immediate risk.

BleepingComputer

GitHub's CodeQL 2.26 adds Kotlin 2.4 support and AI prompt-injection detection

GitHub shipped CodeQL 2.26.0 with support for Kotlin 2.4.0, a new JavaScript/TypeScript query that flags system-prompt injection vulnerabilities, and accuracy improvements across several languages; the update rolls out automatically to GitHub code scanning.

Why it matters — It's one of the first mainstream static-analysis checks aimed specifically at prompt-injection bugs in AI-integrated code.

GitHub

npm v12 turns off install scripts and remote/git dependencies by default

npm v12, now the default registry client, no longer runs preinstall, install or postinstall scripts (including implicit native builds) or resolves git and remote-URL dependencies unless a project explicitly allows them, closing off a technique used in numerous supply-chain attacks.

Why it matters — Teams that haven't run `npm approve-scripts` yet will see builds start failing until they explicitly allowlist the scripts they trust.

Bitcoin

Coinbase's bitcoin premium stays negative for a record 60 straight days

Bitcoin fell below $63,000 on July 17 as the Coinbase Bitcoin Premium Index extended its negative streak to 60 consecutive days, the longest on record and well past the prior 40-day streak from January-February, a signal analysts read as persistently weak U.S. institutional demand.

Why it matters — A record-long negative premium points to structurally soft U.S. buying pressure even as bitcoin's price has partly recovered from June's lows.

The Block

House CLARITY Act hearing in New York pressures Senate ahead of August recess

The House Financial Services Committee held a field hearing at Federal Hall on July 17 pushing the Senate to pass the CLARITY Act, the bill that would split digital-asset oversight between the SEC and CFTC; the Senate still lacks the seven to nine Democratic votes needed to clear a filibuster, with disputes over insider-trading rules and stablecoin yield unresolved.

Why it matters — This is the last realistic window for crypto market-structure legislation before Congress leaves for August recess.
