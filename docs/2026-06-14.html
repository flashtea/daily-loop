US signals Fable 5 export ban is Anthropic-specific; other frontier labs face no immediate controls

One day after forcing Anthropic to pull Fable 5 and Mythos 5, US government officials told The Information the export-control directive is targeted at those specific models and is unlikely to be extended to other frontier AI companies. The ban was triggered by a claimed jailbreak Anthropic publicly disputes as narrow and non-universal — noting the same prompts elicit comparable outputs from GPT-5.5.

Why it matters — Confirms yesterday's ban is a targeted precedent, not a broader regulatory sweep — material for every frontier lab's deployment planning.

AI Research & Models

Google releases DiffusionGemma: 26B open-weight model generating text 4× faster via parallel diffusion

Google DeepMind released DiffusionGemma on June 10 under Apache 2.0 — a 26B-parameter model that generates full paragraphs simultaneously, image-diffusion-style, rather than token-by-token. It reaches 700+ tokens/s on an RTX 5090 and 1,000+ on a single H100, with support already merged into vLLM, MLX, and Hugging Face Transformers.

Why it matters — First open-weight production diffusion text model: trades some benchmark accuracy for a 4× throughput multiplier, with day-one inference-stack support.

AI for Software Development

OpenAI retires GPT-5.2 across ChatGPT and API; all users now on GPT-5.5

As of June 12, GPT-5.2 Instant, Thinking, and Pro were removed from ChatGPT; existing conversations auto-migrate to the corresponding GPT-5.5 variant. API users have a 90-day deprecation window from GPT-5.5's launch before endpoint access is cut.

Why it matters — Developers with fine-tuned prompts, evals, or agentic pipelines targeting GPT-5.2 behavior should validate against GPT-5.5 before the API cutoff.

Bitcoin.com News

Bitcoin Core v31.0 discloses privacy bug: -privatebroadcast feature can expose node IP addresses

Bitcoin Core developers disclosed on June 6 that the optional -privatebroadcast feature in v31.0 — designed to route transactions privately via Tor — can reveal the originating node's IP address due to a BIP324 v2 handshake failure. A fix is targeted for v31.1; users who enabled the feature should disable it immediately.

Why it matters — Only v31.0 operators who opted into -privatebroadcast are at risk, but anyone relying on that feature for transaction anonymity should act before v31.1 ships.
