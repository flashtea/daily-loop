Amazon CEO Andy Jassy called the White House the night before the Fable 5 export ban — models enter day four offline

Amazon security researchers identified what they described as a dangerous exploit in Claude Fable 5 and briefed CEO Andy Jassy, who then personally contacted Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and other senior White House officials on the evening of June 11 — directly preceding the Commerce Department's export-control directive to Anthropic the following afternoon. Both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain suspended globally. Prediction markets give 78% odds US-only access returns by July 1. The timing is especially damaging for Anthropic, which filed an S-1 confidentially for an October listing at a $965B valuation on June 1.

Why it matters — Amazon is simultaneously Anthropic's largest investor, primary cloud infrastructure, and now the entity that triggered a government shutdown of its most capable deployed model — a structural conflict enterprises should factor into AI vendor strategy.

npm v12 preview: install scripts off by default, git dependencies blocked — test your pipelines now

GitHub's npm team announced upcoming breaking changes for npm v12 (targeting July 2026) and made them available behind warnings in npm 11.17.0. Key changes: allowScripts defaults to false, blocking preinstall/postinstall scripts from any dependency without explicit opt-in; and --allow-git defaults to none, blocking git-URL dependencies. Teams can test both flags today in npm 11.17.0.

Why it matters — Projects relying on package install scripts — common in AI SDK chains, native addons, and build tooling — need to audit and add explicit allow-lists before npm v12 ships.

Botanix Bitcoin L2 winds down July 9 after four years; TVL was $119K at announcement

Polychain-backed Botanix — a tokenless EVM layer-2 built on Bitcoin that launched mainnet in 2025 — announced it is shutting down after failing to generate sustainable fee revenue. At announcement, TVL stood at just $119,500. The post-mortem cited that most users treated Botanix as a quiet yield-earning vault rather than a high-frequency transaction layer, making fee income insufficient to cover operating costs. Why it matters — A data point against the Bitcoin DeFi thesis: unless L2 activity shifts from yield farming to genuine high-frequency use, fee revenue cannot sustain these networks.
