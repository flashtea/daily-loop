Anthropic makes Claude Fable 5 a permanent, rationed feature — and pushes Pro users to pay-per-token

Starting today, Fable 5 is included in Max and Team Premium plans at 50% of already-reduced weekly limits, while Pro and Team Standard subscribers lose bundled access entirely, getting a one-time $100 credit before falling back to API pricing of $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens — the steepest rate of any Claude model.

Why it matters — It ends five weeks of extend-and-relaunch churn on Anthropic's flagship model and effectively meters Claude Code's most capable model for everyone but Max/Team Premium subscribers.

AI Research & Models

Anthropic in early talks to lease up to $10 billion in compute from Meta

Anthropic proposed the arrangement in June and Meta is now evaluating it; if finalized, Anthropic would pay Meta monthly across a two-year term, with either side able to walk away early. The deal would be smaller than Anthropic's $45 billion, three-year compute agreement with SpaceX signed in May.

Why it matters — It's a direct read on how compute-constrained Anthropic is right now — the same constraint behind this week's Fable 5 rationing — and a new revenue angle for Meta's AI infrastructure buildout.

CNBC / Yahoo Finance

Chip stocks confirm a bear market as the AI rally unravels

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index closed 20.2% below its late-June record on July 17, erasing roughly $3.3 trillion in global chip-stock value; Intel, Marvell and Arm are each down more than 30% from their highs, and Micron is down 30%. Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 release, which narrowed China's gap with US frontier models, is cited as the proximate trigger for renewed doubt about US AI capex returns.

Why it matters — The SOX had rallied 105% since March on AI-buildout optimism; a confirmed bear market is the market formally questioning whether that spending pays off, with Alphabet's July 22 earnings seen as the next test.

Dev Tools & Releases

GitHub Actions starts enforcing checkout protections against 'pwn request' attacks

Enforcement of a June security update to actions/checkout — which refuses to run when triggered by common pull_request_target 'pwn request' patterns — begins today for all supported backported versions, after the rollout was pushed back from July 16. Workflows pinned to a floating major tag pick up the change automatically; those pinned to a specific SHA or minor version need to upgrade manually.

Why it matters — pull_request_target is one of the most commonly abused GitHub Actions triggers; workflows that don't upgrade could start failing today, but ones left unpatched stay exposed to a well-known attacker-controlled-code pattern.

Microsoft preps 'Project Perception,' an AI bug-fixing tool to rival Anthropic's Mythos

Microsoft is finishing an AI-powered cyber-defense product, internally codenamed Project Perception, that routes vulnerability-scanning and fix-suggestion tasks across models from Anthropic, OpenAI and Microsoft itself to keep costs well below Anthropic's Mythos-based offering. It's expected to launch as soon as this month.

Why it matters — It's Microsoft entering the AI-driven vulnerability-remediation market Anthropic effectively created with Mythos, betting a cheaper multi-model router beats a single frontier model on cost.

Bitcoin

CLARITY Act's merged Senate draft drops the ethics provision Democrats demanded, threatening this week's floor vote

A merged Banking and Agriculture Committee draft released last week omits the Section 604 conflict-of-interest language barring senior officials from holding crypto interests while in office; Senators Murphy, Van Hollen and Merkley have formally come out against the bill without it. Majority Leader Thune still wants a floor vote the week of July 20, but without that language the bill likely can't clear the 60-vote threshold before the August recess.

Why it matters — This is the last realistic window before Congress leaves for August recess; missing it likely pushes crypto market-structure legislation into 2027.

Traders put $2.5 billion on bitcoin hitting $72,000 by month-end, timed to the Fed's July 29 decision

Large Deribit traders bought 20,000 bull call spreads (long the $70,000 strike, short the $72,000 strike) expiring July 31, betting the Fed's July 29 rate decision — where markets favor a hold at 3.5%-3.75% — will be the catalyst. Bitcoin was trading near $64,700 when the trades were placed.

Why it matters — It's a concrete, sized bet that ties bitcoin's next leg to a specific macro catalyst rather than crypto-native news.

CoinDesk

Kraken launches USD-settled bitcoin and ether options with no crypto collateral required

Kraken began offering cash-settled BTC and ETH options via request-for-quote on Kraken Pro, letting institutional traders handle premiums, P&L and settlement entirely in dollars instead of managing crypto collateral; a public order book and European availability are planned next.

Why it matters — Kraken is betting that simpler product design, not weak demand, has kept crypto options a niche market compared to equities and FX.
