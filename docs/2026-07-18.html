AI Research & Models

Kimi K3's launch triggers a global AI-valuation selloff in chip stocks

Moonshot AI's 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight Kimi K3 -- released July 16 alongside a TSMC earnings report flagging higher-than-expected capex -- rekindled investor scrutiny of US AI infrastructure spending; the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell roughly 11% on the week, Nvidia dropped about 3.4%, and AMD and Applied Materials each fell 5% or more.

Why it matters — A Chinese lab matching top US models' benchmark scores, in the open, is now moving hundreds of billions of dollars in US tech market cap -- not just AI-industry sentiment.

AI for Software Development

Kimi K3 tops Arena's Frontend Code leaderboard, ahead of Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6

Within hours of its debut, Moonshot AI's open-weight Kimi K3 climbed to No. 1 on Arena.ai's Frontend Code Arena, scoring 1,679 versus Claude Fable 5's 1,631 and GPT-5.6 Sol's 1,618; Moonshot also shipped two point releases of its Kimi Code coding agent the same day.

Why it matters — It's the first time an open-weight model has led a major real-world coding benchmark ahead of both leading closed US models.

The New Stack

Claude Code adds background /fork sessions and runaway-loop limits

Anthropic's July 17 Claude Code update makes /fork copy a conversation into a new background session while you keep working (the in-session version is now /subtask), and adds default session caps of 200 WebSearch calls and 200 subagent spawns to stop runaway search or delegation loops; long-running MCP tool calls now move to the background automatically.

Why it matters — The new caps directly target a class of bugs where unbounded subagent fan-out or search loops burn tokens without a developer noticing.

Anthropic

GitHub Copilot usage metrics go repository-level

GitHub's Copilot usage metrics REST API now exposes two new endpoints returning a daily, per-repository breakdown of pull request activity for Copilot's coding agent and Copilot code review, on top of the org- and team-level views already available.

Why it matters — Engineering leaders can now see which specific repos are actually benefiting from Copilot, rather than relying on org-wide averages.

AI Research & Models

Xi Jinping opens Shanghai's WAIC, unveils a 29-nation AI governance body

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference opened in Shanghai on July 17 with President Xi Jinping's first-ever in-person appearance at the event; 29 countries signed an agreement establishing a new World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization, and more than 300 products made their global premiere among 1,100-plus exhibiting companies.

Why it matters — It's Beijing's most visible push yet to position itself as a co-author of global AI governance rules rather than just a fast-follower on models.

Dev Tools & Releases

PostgreSQL 19 Beta 2 released

The PostgreSQL Global Development Group shipped the second beta of PostgreSQL 19 on July 16, the latest feature preview ahead of a general-availability release expected around September or October 2026.

Why it matters — Beta 2 is the point where the project wants real application workloads tested, not just syntax checks, before the feature set locks.

GitHub Projects ships advanced AND/OR search, now generally available

The filter bar in GitHub Projects views now supports advanced search expressions using AND and OR logic instead of requiring a separate saved view for every question, and pull request items can now be filtered by review state with a new reviews: qualifier.

Why it matters — It closes a long-standing gap between GitHub Projects and dedicated project-tracking tools that have supported compound filters for years.

Bitcoin

Bitcoin slides below $63,000 as fresh Iran strikes and the AI selloff hit risk assets

Bitcoin fell below $63,000 on July 17 as new US airstrikes on Iran, a fresh US-China trade dispute, and the AI-valuation reassessment sweeping chip and tech stocks pushed investors out of risk assets broadly; the decline extended a 1.4% slide from the prior session and left BTC roughly 5% off its recent highs.

Why it matters — It shows bitcoin trading as a risk asset alongside tech stocks this week rather than decoupling from equity-market stress.

CoinDesk

Bitcoin ETFs post a third straight day of inflows, led by BlackRock's IBIT

US spot bitcoin ETFs pulled in $79.15 million on July 16, extending a three-day inflow streak to about $368 million and snapping an eight-week outflow run of more than $8 billion; BlackRock's IBIT led with $33.44 million, followed by Fidelity's FBTC at $30.73 million.

Why it matters — It's the clearest sign yet that institutional demand was stabilizing just before Friday's broader risk-off wave hit price.
