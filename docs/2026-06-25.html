GitHub Copilot ships a standalone desktop app for agentic coding workflows

GitHub's Copilot app reached general availability on June 17, giving developers a dedicated desktop home for multi-step agent sessions — outside the IDE. It exposes sandbox environments, multi-turn planning, and the same agent runtime as the Copilot extension, with support for MCP integrations.

Dev Tools & Releases

TypeScript 7.0 RC ships with a Go-native compiler delivering ~10× faster builds

Microsoft published the TypeScript 7.0 Release Candidate on June 18. The compiler is a port of the TypeScript codebase to Go — not a rewrite — so type-checking semantics are identical, but teams at Figma, Vercel, Linear, Notion and others report build times dropping by a majority. Stable GA is targeted roughly one month out.

Why it matters — For any large TypeScript monorepo, a 10× type-check speedup is the biggest productivity improvement the language has offered in years.

Microsoft TypeScript Blog

GitHub Copilot SDK hits GA across 6 languages with MCP and BYOK support

The Copilot SDK reached general availability on June 2 in Node/TypeScript, Python, Go, .NET, Rust, and Java, exposing the same agent runtime — planning, tool invocation, file edits, streaming — that powers the Copilot app. Developers can plug in their own LLM keys via BYOK and wire up any MCP server.

Why it matters — Lets teams embed Copilot's agentic engine directly into internal tools and pipelines without building their own orchestration layer.

Bitcoin

Bitcoin slides toward $60K as spot ETFs log sixth consecutive week of outflows totalling $5.94B

BTC traded as low as $60,669 on June 24, down more than 3% on the day, after confirming a bear flag breakdown on the daily chart. Spot Bitcoin ETFs have shed $5.94 billion over six straight weeks of net outflows — the largest cumulative drawdown since spot ETF approval — though June 23 saw a brief $39.2M inflow led by ARKB, too small to break the trend.

Why it matters — Six weeks of institutional selling erases the 'ETF flows equal permanent bid' narrative that drove May's rally to $77K.

CoinDesk

CLARITY Act reaches Senate floor calendar; August recess is now the hard deadline

The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act — which would give the CFTC exclusive jurisdiction over digital commodity spot markets and resolve the SEC/CFTC turf war — was placed on the Senate Legislative Calendar on June 1, making it eligible for a full floor vote. Senate staff say it must clear the chamber before the August recess to have any realistic shot at becoming law this year; it still needs 60 votes and reconciliation with the House version.

Why it matters — The first comprehensive US crypto market structure law in years — passage would give Bitcoin spot markets a clear regulatory framework for the first time.
