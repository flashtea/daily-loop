MiCA's grace period ends today: Binance locked out of the EU, ~14 licensed exchanges absorb the volume

The EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets transitional period expires July 1 with no extension. Binance, MEXC, HTX and Bitfinex have no MiCA authorization and must stop onboarding EU clients immediately; France alone had identified roughly 90 unlicensed operators ahead of the deadline. Around 14 exchanges — including Coinbase (Luxembourg), Kraken (Ireland), OKX, Crypto.com and Bybit — hold licenses and already handle an estimated 95% of EU crypto volume. Tether's USDT remains excluded from regulated EU markets.

Why it matters — The largest hard regulatory cutover in crypto history takes effect today, reshaping which exchanges can legally serve the EU's crypto users overnight.

Dev Tools & Releases

Docker Desktop 4.80.0 patches a VM-panic kernel bug and bumps Kubernetes to 1.36.1

Released June 29, Docker Desktop 4.80.0 fixes CVE-2026-8936 — a VM panic caused by unbounded recursion in the grpcfuse kernel module when containers create deeply nested directories on bind-mounted host folders — and updates the bundled Kubernetes to v1.36.1 along with CNI plugins, cri-tools and cri-dockerd.

Why it matters — The grpcfuse bug could crash the Docker Desktop VM from inside an untrusted container; teams running Docker Desktop should update before hitting it in CI or local dev.

US spot Bitcoin ETFs close their worst month on record: $4.06B in June outflows

US spot Bitcoin ETFs logged about $4.06 billion in net outflows in June, the largest monthly redemption since the products launched in January 2024, exceeding the prior record of $3.56 billion set in February 2025. BlackRock's IBIT accounted for roughly $3.3 billion of the month's total, about 75% of the outflow from a single fund.

Why it matters — A record monthly redemption from the primary institutional access point for Bitcoin signals sustained, not one-off, institutional de-risking heading into July.
