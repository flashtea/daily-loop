UK AI Security Institute finds "universal jailbreaks" in GPT-5.6's cyber safeguards

The UK's AI Security Institute says it built jailbreaks -- some within hours -- that strip away GPT-5.6's safeguards against cyberattacks, unlocking autonomous vulnerability discovery and exploit development rather than just information about flaws. AISI called the findings more severe than a comparable jailbreak found in Anthropic's Fable 5, and expects further red-teaming to surface similar bypasses despite OpenAI's mitigations.

Why it matters — A government lab demonstrating hours-long jailbreaks to autonomous hacking capability, right after the model's cyber risk forced a government-vetted rollout, undercuts the premise of that gated launch.

NYT and 16 other publishers seek sanctions against OpenAI over withheld evidence

The New York Times, the New York Daily News and 15 other news organizations asked a federal court to sanction OpenAI, alleging a deposition showed the company had already built and used a database of roughly 78 million de-identified ChatGPT conversations to gauge its own copyright exposure while claiming it couldn't search chat logs for infringement. Critical Gitea Docker auth-bypass under active exploitation, ~6,200 instances exposed

Attackers are exploiting CVE-2026-20896, a 9.8-severity flaw in Gitea's official Docker image that trusts an X-WEBAUTH-USER header from any source IP when reverse-proxy authentication is enabled, letting anyone who can reach the port log in as any user -- including admins -- with no password. Roughly 6,200 internet-facing instances remain vulnerable; Gitea 1.26.3+ fixes it by making that trust opt-in.

Why it matters — Self-hosted Gitea is a popular GitHub alternative for teams and open-source projects, and this flaw hands over full admin access with a single forged header.

Bitcoin and ether ETFs snap an eight-week, $9.46B outflow streak

US spot bitcoin and ether ETFs pulled in a combined $281.8M for the week ending July 11 -- $197.4M into bitcoin funds and $84.4M into ether funds -- ending the longest outflow streak since the products launched in January 2024. BlackRock's IBIT and ETHA led Friday's inflows while most other issuers saw no net flows; both asset classes remain deeply underwater for the year.

Why it matters — A single positive week recovers only about 3% of the prior outflows, so it's too early to call this a trend reversal rather than a pause.
