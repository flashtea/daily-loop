Anthropic, Amazon, Microsoft and Google propose a shared scale for rating AI jailbreak severity

Anthropic published a five-tier "Cyber Jailbreak Severity" scale, developed with its Glasswing partners including Amazon, Microsoft and Google, that rates jailbreak techniques from informational to critical based on capability gain, ease of weaponization and discoverability.

Why it matters — The first attempt at an industry-standard vocabulary for jailbreak risk, meant to let competing labs and governments compare notes on AI safety incidents consistently.

AI for Software Development

Godot Engine bans nearly all AI-generated code from contributions

The Godot game engine's maintainers rewrote their contribution policy to prohibit AI-authored code beyond menial assistance like completion or regex, auto-banning contributors who submit autonomous-agent or "vibe-coded" pull requests without disclosure.

Why it matters — One of open source's most-used engines concluding that AI PRs are net-negative for maintainer time is a notable data point as more projects weigh the same tradeoff.

Anthropic quietly strips hidden Claude Code tracking aimed at rival AI labs

Anthropic removed steganographic checks it had built into Claude Code in March that compared a user's hostname against a hidden list of Chinese AI labs and unauthorized resellers, put in place to curb account abuse and model distillation.

Why it matters — A reminder that widely-trusted developer tools can carry undisclosed telemetry, and that removing it only came after developers noticed.

Bitcoin experts split over a plan to freeze Satoshi's 1.1 million BTC against quantum theft

A proposal that would eventually freeze quantum-vulnerable addresses — including roughly 1.1 million bitcoin attributed to Satoshi Nakamoto — is dividing the developer community between preserving strict immutability and pre-emptively protecting dormant coins.

Why it matters — Any move to freeze addresses, even quantum-vulnerable ones, cuts against bitcoin's core neutrality premise and would be its most consequential protocol debate in years.
