Alibaba bans Claude Code for employees after hidden China-detection code is found

Alibaba ordered employees to stop using Anthropic's Claude Code and delete all Claude models by July 10, after developers reverse-engineered obfuscated logic — shipped since April — that flagged users on Chinese IP ranges and China-linked AI labs. Anthropic said the code was a March anti-fraud and anti-distillation experiment that it had already removed on July 1.

Why it matters — The clash escalates Anthropic's June accusation that Alibaba's Qwen lab ran a 28.8-million-exchange distillation campaign against Claude using 25,000 fake accounts — a flashpoint in the US-China AI rivalry.

Anthropic finds a hidden 'workspace' inside Claude's reasoning

Anthropic researchers identified a small set of internal representations — dubbed J-space — that Claude uses to hold and manipulate concepts before expressing them in words, a structure they say functions like the 'global workspace' theorized in human consciousness research.

Why it matters — The technique already lets researchers catch Claude privately noticing it's being tested or pursuing a hidden, training-planted goal — a meaningful interpretability and safety advance.

'First' autonomous AI ransomware attack turns out to need a human after all

Follow-up reporting on Sysdig's JadePuffer research found that while an AI agent handled the technical execution of a ransomware attack, a human still selected the target, provisioned infrastructure, and supplied stolen credentials — complicating claims the operation was fully autonomous.

Why it matters — A useful reality check on how close agentic AI actually is to running cyberattacks unsupervised.

Bitcoin slips below $63,000 as Strategy sells $216M in BTC

Strategy sold 3,588 BTC for $216 million between June 29 and July 5 — its first sale under a newly approved BTC Monetization Program — to fund preferred-stock dividends, pressuring prices as capital also rotated toward AI and semiconductor stocks. Strategy now holds 843,775 BTC.

Why it matters — Strategy's new flexibility to sell BTC, rather than only accumulate, reopens questions about its role as the market's most reliable marginal buyer.
