Altman, Amodei, and Hassabis appear together at G7 for the first time

OpenAI's Sam Altman, Anthropic's Dario Amodei, and Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis all attended the 52nd G7 summit in Évian-les-Bains, France (June 15–17) — the first time all three rival frontier-lab chiefs have appeared jointly before heads of state. The agenda centred on AI infrastructure investment and voluntary safety commitments, with youth safety high on Altman's personal list.

Why it matters — Whatever voluntary framework emerges sets the baseline for how labs engage with governments globally heading into a crowded second half of 2026.

Gemini CLI access ends June 18 — migrate to Antigravity CLI today

Google cuts off Gemini CLI and Gemini Code Assist IDE extensions for free, Pro, and Ultra accounts on Wednesday June 18. The replacement, Antigravity CLI, is a new Go-based multi-agent terminal that routes tasks through Google's unified Antigravity agent backend and supports parallel background sessions.

Why it matters — Any developer using Gemini CLI on a personal plan loses access tomorrow; Standard and Enterprise licensees are unaffected but should test the new CLI.

Anthropic reverses the June 15 Agent SDK billing split

Anthropic confirmed it will not proceed with the planned change that would have billed claude -p, Agent SDK, and third-party app calls against a separate monthly credit pool outside subscription limits. Users stay on their existing billing setup; the reversal follows community pushback from developers running production agents.

Why it matters — Any integrations restructured in anticipation of the split can revert; no new line item on the June billing cycle.

Linux kernel 7.1 ships with rewritten NTFS driver and Intel FRED as new default

Linus Torvalds released Linux 7.1 on June 14, featuring a new in-kernel NTFS driver with full read/write support, Intel FRED (Flexible Return and Event Delivery) replacing legacy interrupt delivery on x86, and removal of over 140,000 lines of obsolete 486-era sub-architecture code from more than 300 first-time contributors.

Why it matters — Clears long-standing x86 cruft and improves Windows filesystem interoperability — solid groundwork for the next LTS cycle.
